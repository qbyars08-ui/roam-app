Tokyo, Japan

5 days · Comfort · Hidden gems, Local eats
Day 1 — Arrival & Shinjuku

Get lost on purpose

  • Arrive Narita/Haneda, grab Suica card at the airport
  • Check in at hotel in Shinjuku (walk to Golden Gai)
  • Afternoon: Shinjuku Gyoen for jet-lag reset
  • Evening: Omoide Yokocho for yakitori and cold beer
  • Late night: Golden Gai bar hop (pick the one with 6 seats)
~$85/day
Day 2 — Shibuya & Harajuku

Culture overload (the good kind)

  • Morning: Meiji Shrine before the crowds (go by 8am)
  • Brunch: Harajuku crepe from Marion Crepes
  • Afternoon: Shibuya crossing, then Nonbei Yokocho
  • Evening: Standing sushi bar in Shibuya (follow the salary-men)
  • Night: Shibuya Sky observation deck at sunset
~$70/day
Day 3 — Asakusa & Akihabara

Old meets new

  • Morning: Senso-ji temple (arrive 7am for empty photos)
  • Lunch: Tempura at Daikokuya (cash only, worth the wait)
  • Afternoon: Akihabara arcades and retro game shops
  • Evening: Ramen at Fuunji (tsukemen style, Shinjuku)
  • Night: Robot Restaurant or TeamLab Borderless
~$90/day
Day 4 — Tsukiji & Ginza

Eat everything

  • Morning: Tsukiji Outer Market (tamagoyaki, fresh uni)
  • Lunch: Sushi Dai or Daiwa Sushi (queue early)
  • Afternoon: Ginza window shopping, Itoya stationery
  • Evening: Izakaya dinner in Yurakucho under the tracks
  • Night: Ginza whiskey bar (Hibiki 17 if they have it)
~$110/day
Day 5 — Day Trip or Departure

One more for the road

  • Option A: Day trip to Kamakura (Great Buddha + beach)
  • Option B: Shimokitazawa vintage shops + coffee
  • Lunch: Final bowl of ramen (dealer's choice)
  • Pack up, Narita Express to airport
~$75/day
