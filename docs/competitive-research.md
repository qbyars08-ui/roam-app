# ROAM Competitive Intelligence Report: Top 10 Travel Apps (2025-2026)

*Generated March 2026*

## Top 5 Gaps ROAM Should Own

### 1. The "Planning is 12 Apps" Problem
Every existing travel app does ONE thing. Users bounce between Booking (hotels), Skyscanner (flights), Google Maps (navigation), TripAdvisor (reviews), Google Translate (language), TripIt (organization). Nobody connects inspiration to execution in one place.

**ROAM advantage:** We're the only app that generates a complete plan — accommodation, activities, food, budget, packing, language — in 60 seconds flat.

### 2. Nobody Advocates for the Traveler When Things Go Wrong
The #1 complaint across ALL travel apps: refunds, cancellations, overbookings, and customer support black holes. Booking.com, Airbnb, Expedia, Hopper — all have refund horror stories as their top 1-star reviews.

**ROAM opportunity:** Emergency SOS already exists. Build a "Trip Shield" feature — proactive alerts when flights change, backup accommodation suggestions, and direct emergency guidance.

### 3. AI Planning Exists But Is Unreliable and Generic
Only 7% of travelers trust AI-generated travel recommendations (Skift Research 2025). Existing AI tools produce generic, corporate-sounding itineraries. Users don't trust "explore the local culture" — they want "order the #3 at the counter."

**ROAM advantage:** Our system prompt is specifically designed to be opinionated and specific. We name real restaurants, real dishes, real prices. This is our #1 differentiator.

### 4. Group Trip Planning Is Completely Broken
No major travel app handles group coordination well. Friends use Google Docs, group chats, shared spreadsheets. Trip Chemistry exists in ROAM but could be expanded to full group planning.

**ROAM opportunity:** Group trip mode with shared itineraries, voting on activities, and split budgets.

### 5. No App Connects Inspiration to Execution (TikTok-to-Boarding-Pass Gap)
Users see incredible travel content on TikTok/Instagram but have no way to turn "I want to go there" into an actual plan. The gap between inspiration and booking is still 3+ hours of manual research.

**ROAM advantage:** Spin the Globe + instant itinerary generation. User sees a destination → 60 seconds later has a full trip plan.

## App-by-App Findings

### 1. Booking.com (76.5M downloads/yr, 4.8 iOS)
- Refunds promised but never processed
- Confirmed reservations not honored on arrival
- Customer service impossible to reach
- Users want: A platform that advocates for them

### 2. Airbnb (57.6M downloads/yr, 4.8 iOS)
- Refunds issued as credits, not real money
- Property conditions don't match photos
- Host cancellations leave guests stranded
- Users want: Verified conditions, guaranteed backup accommodation

### 3. Google Maps (billions, 4.7 iOS)
- No trip planning integration
- Battery drain, loud notifications
- Fake review epidemic
- Users want: Organized itinerary from saved pins

### 4. TripAdvisor (20M+ downloads/yr, 4.7 iOS)
- Booking engine reserves wrong dates
- Reviews suppressed without explanation
- Fake/incentivized ratings growing
- Users want: Curated recs, not endless browsing

### 5. Expedia (21.5M downloads/yr, 4.8 iOS)
- Double charges, hidden fees (+50% on car rentals)
- Flights changed without consent
- Users want: Price transparency, proactive notifications

### 6. Hopper (18.7M downloads/yr, 4.7 iOS)
- Bait-and-switch pricing
- Price Freeze deposits not honored
- No human support available
- Users want: Honest locked-in pricing

### 7. Skyscanner (4.8 iOS)
- Prices $40-$220 more than airline websites
- Denies responsibility for booking issues
- Users want: End-to-end ownership

### 8. Kayak (4.8 iOS)
- Unvetted third-party vendors running scams
- Prices change on redirect
- Users want: Vetted vendors, fallback options

### 9. Google Translate (4.7 iOS)
- Camera translation degraded in recent updates
- Voice translation cuts out mid-sentence
- Users want: Context-aware travel translation (menus, signs)

### 10. TripIt (4.8 iOS)
- Email parsing fails for 1/3 of bookings
- Constant sign-out loops at airports
- Users want: Reliable trip organization that works offline
