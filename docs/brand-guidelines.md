# ROAM — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

ROAM is the AI travel planner that sounds like your most well-traveled friend. We are knowledgeable, opinionated, specific, and never boring. We don't sound like a brochure, a chatbot, or a travel agency. We sound like someone who's been there.

---

## Logo

### Wordmark
The ROAM wordmark is set in **Cormorant Garamond Bold** with **6px letter-spacing**. It is always displayed in Gold (#C9A84C) on dark backgrounds or Dark Green (#0D1F1A) on light backgrounds.

### Usage Rules
- Minimum size: 24px font size
- Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the "O" character on all sides
- Never stretch, skew, rotate, or add effects to the wordmark
- Never place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Never change the font or letter-spacing

### Logo Variations
| Variation | Color | Background |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Primary | Gold (#C9A84C) | Dark Green (#0D1F1A) |
| Light | Dark Green (#0D1F1A) | White or Cream |
| Monochrome | White (#FFFFFF) | Any dark background |

### Color Rules
- Dark Green is always the base background. ROAM is a dark-mode-only product.
- Sage is the primary accent. Use for active tab icons, progress bars, success states, and interactive elements.
- Coral is reserved for CTAs and warnings. Do not overuse.
- Gold is the premium accent. Use for the ROAM wordmark, passport badge borders, and earned achievements.
- Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) for text. Always use Cream (#F5EDD8).
- Never use pure black (#000000) for backgrounds. Always use Dark Green (#0D1F1A).

- Line height for headers: 1.15-1.25

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
ROAM sounds like your friend who's been everywhere and always knows a spot.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **Specific** | Name the place, the dish, the street | "Order the #3 at Jay Fai" not "try local food" |
| **Opinionated** | Have a point of view, make a recommendation | "Skip the tourist restaurants on the main strip" |
| **Honest** | Acknowledge downsides, don't oversell | "March is shoulder season — some places close, but no crowds" |
| **Warm** | Friendly but not performative | "Your passport is empty — plan your first trip" not "Welcome! Let's get started on your travel journey!" |
| **Concise** | Say it in fewer words | "Neon nights, temple mornings" not "Tokyo offers a vibrant blend of modern culture and ancient tradition" |

### Tone Do's
- Sound like a text from a friend
- Be direct and specific
- Use contractions naturally
- Include insider knowledge
- Be honest about trade-offs

### Tone Don'ts
- Never sound like a brochure or press release
- Never use "explore" as a verb by itself ("explore the area")
- Never use "consider visiting" or "you may want to"
- Never use corporate phrases ("diverse culinary landscape")
- Never use exclamation marks excessively
- Never say "hidden gem" unironically
- Never start sentences with "Whether you're..."

### Writing Examples

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Explore the rich culinary landscape" | "$5 meals that change your life" |
| "Consider visiting during off-peak hours" | "Get there by 3pm or you're waiting 45 min" |
| "A diverse selection of activities" | "Temples at dawn, street food at midnight" |
| "Your one-stop travel solution" | "Your next trip, planned in minutes" |
| "We're excited to help you plan!" | "Where to next?" |

## Photography Style

When using destination photography in the app or marketing materials:
- Prefer moody, atmospheric shots over bright tourist photos
- Golden hour and blue hour lighting
- Street-level perspectives over aerial/drone shots
- Show real life — street vendors, local markets, lived-in spaces
- Always apply a dark gradient overlay for text readability (transparent to rgba(0,0,0,0.65))
- Avoid stock photo aesthetics — no staged smiles, no suitcase-on-a-beach

## UI Patterns

### Cards
- Background: `rgba(255,255,255,0.05)`
- Border: 1px `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)`
- Border radius: 16px (lg)
- Padding: 24px (lg)

### Buttons — Primary
- Background: Sage (#7CAF8A) or Sage gradient
- Text: Dark Green (#0D1F1A), DM Sans Bold
- Border radius: 16px
- Padding: 18px vertical

### Buttons — CTA/Upgrade
- Background: Coral (#E8614A)
- Text: White or Dark Green
- Reserved for upgrade prompts and primary CTAs only

### Buttons — Outline
- Background: transparent
- Border: 1px Cream Muted
- Text: Cream

### Input Fields
- Background: Glass BG
- Border: 1px Border color, Sage on focus
- Text: Cream
- Placeholder: Cream Muted
- Border radius: 12px
