## 1. The Austrian Founder Advantage

### Why It Matters

- **EU founder status** — Not a US tourist trying to break into Europe. Quinn holds Austrian citizenship. Can operate an EU entity natively, attend EU startup events, access EU grants, and build relationships without visa friction.

- **German fluency** — Can speak directly to the market, review creator content for quality, approve German copy, and engage with DACH users in their language. Most US founders cannot do this.

- **Cultural credibility** — Austrian passport = instant trust in DACH. "Ein Österreicher baut eine Reise-App" (An Austrian builds a travel app) is a narrative that resonates. Ski culture, Interrail, Alpine travel — Quinn understands the market from the inside.

- **DACH-specific network** — Austrian ski clubs, university travel groups, Erasmus networks. Quinn's natural network is DACH. Authentic connection beats cold outreach.

## 2. EU Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Validation (Month 1-3)

- Validate with organic DACH content and paid UGC
- Test conversion: DACH signup → trial → paid
- Operate under existing US entity or sole proprietor
- No EU entity needed yet — testing product-market fit

### Phase 2: Entity Formation (Month 3-6)

- If traction is real (100+ DACH paid users), register **Estonian e-Residency**
- Estonian OÜ (LLC): ~$200 application, €0.01 minimum share capital
- Route European App Store and subscription revenue through EU entity
- Open Wise or Mercury EU bank account
- **Why Estonia over Austrian GmbH:** Austrian GmbH requires €35K minimum. Estonian OÜ is fully digital, no physical office, ~€500/year maintenance vs €2K+ for Austrian GmbH.

### Phase 3: Grants & Tax (Month 6+)

- **Austria:** aws.at (Austria Wirtschaftsservice) — multiple programs for tech startups
- **Germany:** EXIST (exist.de) — up to €150K for tech startups with university connection
- **Austria:** FFG grants for AI companies (Forschungsförderungsgesellschaft)
- **EU:** Horizon Europe grants for AI/travel tech
- VAT registration when crossing €10K threshold in EU digital sales

## 6. Competitive Moat in DACH

1. **First mover** — No AI travel app actively marketing in German. Window is open.
2. **Local network** — German-speaking travelers matching with German-speaking travelers = DACH-specific network US apps cannot replicate.
3. **Trust** — Austrian founder + EU entity = GDPR-native, data stays in EU. US apps face skepticism.
4. **Content moat** — Once ROAM establishes presence on German TikTok, competitors must outspend to displace — and they won't bother with a "small" market while chasing the US.
