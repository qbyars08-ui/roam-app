## Full Description

Every travel app gives you the same 10 results Google already knows. "Visit the Eiffel Tower. Check out Times Square." Thanks, I had no idea.

ROAM works differently. Tell it where you're going, your budget, and your travel style — solo backpacker, couple on a long weekend, group trip where nobody agrees on anything — and it builds a full day-by-day itinerary in about 30 seconds. We're talking specific neighborhoods, not just city names. Actual restaurant picks with prices in both local currency and yours. Transit directions that say "take the Yamanote Line to Shibuya, exit Hachiko side, 180 yen" instead of "convenient public transportation options."

The itineraries have structure that makes sense for how people actually travel. Mornings start slow. The energy builds. You get a late-afternoon break because you're a human, not a checklist. And the voice adapts — a solo trip to Bangkok reads completely different from a couples' weekend in Charleston. We banned every word travel bloggers have ruined. No "bustling." No "world-class." No "hidden gem" that 4 million people visited last year.

One free trip a month. Pro for unlimited. That's it. No ads, no affiliate hotel links, no "sponsored experiences." Just a plan that's actually worth screenshotting.

## Maker Comment

I built ROAM because I spent 6 hours planning a 4-day trip to Tokyo last year. Six hours across Reddit threads, blog posts that were secretly hotel ads, and Google Maps tabs I kept losing. Then I got there and half my "plan" didn't make sense because I didn't account for transit time between neighborhoods.

The breaking point was reading a travel blog that described Shinjuku as a "vibrant tapestry of modern culture." That sentence has zero information in it. It tells you nothing. I could swap "Shinjuku" for any city on earth and it'd still technically be correct.

So I started building something that gives you the stuff you actually need — which train, which exit, how much it costs, what's actually nearby, and whether the timing makes sense as a real day a real person would have. Not a listicle. A plan.

ROAM runs on Claude and costs me money every time someone generates a trip, so if you try it and it's useful, that means a lot. And if something's off, tell me — I'm one person and I read every message.

— Quinn
