Last updated: 2026-03-15

## Overview

Three-message sequence targeting German-speaking travel micro-influencers (5K–50K followers) on TikTok and Instagram. All messages in German. Register: casual Gen Z, never corporate. Goal per sequence: get one yes to a 60-second ROAM demo video.

**Target profile:**
- Based in Germany, Austria, or Switzerland
- 18–28 years old
- Travel, lifestyle, or backpacking content
- 5K–50K followers (micro sweet spot)
- Authentic voice, actually travels, engages with comments
- Consistent posting (3+ per week)

**Qualifying search hashtags:**
`#reisetipps` `#backpacking` `#fernweh` `#reisenistleben` `#digitalnomad` `#interrail` `#solotravel` `#reiseplanung` `#urlaubsplanung` `#städtetrip`

## FAQ — Häufige Creator-Fragen & Antworten

**"Was kostet das mich?"**
> Nichts. Die App kostet dich keinen Cent — du kriegst Lifetime Pro. Und wir zahlen dich mit Revenue Share auf Referrals.

**"Was wenn der Content nicht performt?"**
> Dann reden wir drüber. Wir geben dir den Content-Brief, schauen uns die Zahlen an und iterieren. Wir haben 10 getestete Scripts — irgendwas performt immer.

**"Muss ich bestimmte Wörter sagen?"**
> Nein. Wir schicken dir Talking Points — was du zeigst und worüber du redest. Wie du's sagst, ist komplett dir überlassen. Authentizität schlägt immer poliertes Script.

**"Bekomme ich das Geld aus dem Revenue Share wirklich?"**
> Ja. Jeder Signup über deinen UTM-Link wird in PostHog getrackt. Wir zeigen dir den Dashboard-Link, du siehst deine eigenen Zahlen in Echtzeit. Payout monatlich via PayPal oder Banküberweisung.

**"Kann ich auch auf Instagram posten statt TikTok?"**
> Ja — crosspost zu Reels ist erwünscht. Mehr Reichweite = mehr Referrals = mehr Revenue Share für dich.

**"Wie lange ist die Zusammenarbeit?"**
> Kein Vertrag für die ersten Videos. Wir testen das miteinander — wenn's für beide Seiten passt, reden wir über eine langfristigere Zusammenarbeit (und bessere Konditionen).

## Competitor Creator Program Research

*Purpose: Understand what Hopper, Wanderlog, and TripAdvisor offer creators — identify gaps ROAM can exploit in outreach.*

---

### Hopper

**Program type:** Performance-based affiliate / creator network
**Official name:** Hopper has run "creator programs" through its app as part of paid media strategy and via affiliate networks (CJ Affiliate, Impact.com)

**How it works:**
- Creators get a unique deep link or promo code
- Attribution model: last-click install tracking
- Payout: flat rate per attributed app install ($1–$5 per install depending on market)
- Some influencer deals are flat-fee sponsored posts for awareness, not performance-based
- Hopper has also done social commerce experiments with TikTok Shop

**What creators get:**
- Cash per install (one-time, not recurring)
- No equity-style participation in subscription revenue
- No Pro product access (Hopper doesn't have a "creator" tier)
- No real-time dashboard — creators rely on Hopper's attribution report
- No co-marketing or channel feature deals at the micro level

**Weaknesses ROAM exploits:**
1. Pay-per-install is a declining metric — one install does not equal a paying user. A creator who drives 200 installs gets paid $200–$1000 flat. If 10 of those convert to paid subscribers at $9.99/month, that's $1,200/year of MRR the creator helped generate but receives nothing ongoing from.
2. No creator dashboard — creators can't see their own performance in real time. Trust gap.
3. No product access — Hopper can't give creators a "Pro" product to use authentically because Hopper is a booking tool, not a subscription product. ROAM's Lifetime Pro access is a genuine differentiator.
4. Hopper's content format is price alerts, which is boring to film. ROAM's 30-second itinerary generation is visual, surprising, and shareable.

**ROAM outreach angle:** "Hopper pays you once per install. ROAM pays you every month those users stay subscribed. If your audience converts well, you're building a recurring income stream, not a one-time check."

---

### Wanderlog

**Program type:** Minimal — primarily organic/SEO-driven growth, not a creator program
**Official presence:** Wanderlog has an affiliate link on Impact.com and tracks referrals, but no tiered creator program, no structured outreach, no content brief system

**How it works:**
- Standard affiliate link — creator gets a referral code
- Commission: reported at 20–30% of premium subscription revenue
- No structured onboarding, no content briefs, no brand guidelines for creators
- No co-marketing at the creator level
- Growth strategy is mostly B2C SEO (travel itinerary pages indexed by Google) and word-of-mouth among trip planners

**What creators get:**
- Referral link
- Commission on direct conversions
- Nothing else — no product briefings, no story features, no dedicated creator support

**Weaknesses ROAM exploits:**
1. Wanderlog's product is a spreadsheet-style trip planner — not visually content-worthy. There's no "AI generation moment" to film. The experience of using Wanderlog is functional but not entertaining.
2. No creator program infrastructure — no content briefs, no designated creator contact, no community.
3. No social layer. Wanderlog is a solo planning tool. No People tab equivalent, no group trip matching, no shareable trip card designed for Stories format.
4. Wanderlog's strength (Google-indexed itineraries) is invisible to a TikTok or Instagram audience.

**ROAM outreach angle:** "Wanderlog is a planning tool creators use privately. ROAM is a product creators can make content about — the generation is the content."

---

### TripAdvisor

**Program type:** Legacy affiliate / publisher program + occasional influencer deals
**Official program:** TripAdvisor Publisher Program (affiliate), some sponsored posts for brand awareness

**How it works:**
- Publisher (affiliate) program: cost-per-click on hotel/restaurant listings
- CPC rates: $0.15–$0.75 per click to TripAdvisor pages
- Hotel booking commission: 0.5–3% of booking value
- Experiences (tours/activities): higher commission, 5–8% of booking
- Influencer deals: flat-fee sponsored posts for specific campaigns, arranged case-by-case, no standardized creator program

**What creators get:**
- CPC or CPA on third-party inventory (hotels, restaurants, experiences they don't control)
- Payout is tied to user behavior after clicking — complex attribution
- No product to access authentically (TripAdvisor isn't a subscription app with a Pro tier)
- Revenue is CPC — a user who clicks a restaurant listing and then does nothing generates $0.15. A user who books a $300 hotel generates $1.50–$9.00.

**Weaknesses ROAM exploits:**
1. CPC/CPA on third-party inventory aligns creator incentives with TripAdvisor's ad inventory, not with the creator's audience trust. A creator recommending a specific hotel for $0.30 per click looks like (and is) an ad for someone else's business.
2. TripAdvisor's content is reviews and listings — creators can't demo it. "Go look at this restaurant's reviews" is not a TikTok.
3. No AI, no generation moment, no social layer, no shareable native format. TripAdvisor is 20-year-old review infrastructure.
4. Creator earnings are low and unpredictable (depends on user booking behavior).

**ROAM outreach angle:** "TripAdvisor pays you fractions of a cent per click on someone else's hotel listing. ROAM pays you 10–20% of a subscription that a user pays us directly because of you."
