# ROAM — Screenshot Brief

6.7" iPhone (1290 x 2796px) — Primary Set
12.9" iPad (2048 x 2732px) — Adapted from same narrative

All screenshots: dark background (#0A0A0A), no device frames on primary set.
Overlay headers: Space Grotesk Bold, cream (#E8F5E1).
Supporting labels: DM Mono, muted green (#5B9E6F).

---

## Shot 1 — CRAFT Conversation (The Wow Moment)

**Screen to capture:** CRAFT mode mid-conversation. User has typed something like "I want to do 5 days in Tokyo, mid-range budget, I love street food and late nights, not into tourist traps." ROAM is actively responding — a partial itinerary is streaming in below the chat bubble, showing Day 1 taking shape with specific neighborhoods and a restaurant name already visible.

**Visual priority:** Show the conversation input at the top AND the itinerary building below it in the same frame. The simultaneity is the hook — you're talking and the trip is appearing.

**Overlay:**
- Headline: "Plan any trip. Just talk to it."
- Sub-label: "CRAFT mode — your itinerary builds as you speak"

**Purpose:** This is the first screenshot and must carry the full value proposition. A new user should see this and immediately understand: ROAM is different because you talk to it like a person and get a real plan back.

## Shot 2 — Full Itinerary with Real Data

**Screen to capture:** A fully generated itinerary open to a specific day — Day 2 or Day 3 of a trip (shows that depth exists beyond day one). Show a restaurant card with a real photo, star rating, and a one-line context note. Below it, an activity card with a neighborhood label. A small map thumbnail visible. The itinerary feels rich and specific, not generic.

**Visual priority:** Density and specificity. Real photos, real ratings, readable text. This is proof that ROAM's output is actually good.

**Overlay:**
- Headline: "Every restaurant. Every activity. Real data."
- Sub-label: "Google Places, TripAdvisor, Foursquare — all in one plan"

**Purpose:** Overcome the "is this real?" skepticism. Show that ROAM is pulling from real sources, not hallucinating recommendations.

## Shot 3 — I Am Here Now (Night Scene, Tokyo)

**Screen to capture:** I Am Here Now screen. Time shown is ~11:45 PM. Location: Tokyo — a specific neighborhood like Shinjuku or Shimokitazawa visible in the header. ROAM is showing 2–3 options for right now: a late-night ramen spot, a jazz bar, a 24-hour konbini run. Each card has a short reason ("still open," "worth the walk," "locals only"). Emergency tap visible at the bottom as a quiet but present element.

**Visual priority:** The time stamp and the "right now" framing. This screenshot should feel like being in the city at night — it's alive, not static.

**Overlay:**
- Headline: "Your companion at 2AM in a foreign city."
- Sub-label: "I Am Here Now — always knows what's open and worth it"

**Purpose:** Unlock the during-trip use case. Show that ROAM isn't just a pre-trip planner — it's useful the moment you land and every hour after.

## Shot 4 — Prep Tab (Pre-Departure Briefing)

**Screen to capture:** Prep tab showing a pre-departure briefing for an upcoming trip. Visible sections: weather forecast for the destination week, visa status (green checkmark), packing list with a few items checked off, and emergency numbers in a compact card at the bottom. Offline badge visible on the itinerary section — indicates it's been cached.

**Visual priority:** Organized information that feels reassuring, not overwhelming. Clean card layout, clear hierarchy.

**Overlay:**
- Headline: "Everything you need. Before you land."
- Sub-label: "Weather, visa, packing, emergency — all in one place"

**Purpose:** Show the Prep tab as a real differentiator. Pre-trip anxiety is a real user problem. This screenshot says ROAM handles it.

## Shot 5 — Trip Wrapped Summary

**Screen to capture:** Trip Wrapped screen for a completed trip. Show the summary slide view — a full-bleed card style with trip name, destination photo, dates, and a few key stats (days traveled, meals logged, cities visited). A subtle "Share" button visible. The aesthetic should feel like a polished end-of-year recap: editorial, not functional.

**Visual priority:** Visual polish and emotional resonance. This should feel like something worth sharing.

**Overlay:**
- Headline: "Relive every moment."
- Sub-label: "Trip Wrapped — your travels, turned into a story"

**Purpose:** Show the post-trip value loop. Users who see this in the App Store will understand that ROAM has a memory — it's not just a one-trip utility.

## Shot 6 — Dream Board and Savings Goals

**Screen to capture:** Dream tab showing a destination wish list. Two or three destination cards pinned: one with a photo of Lisbon, one of Kyoto, one of a beach destination. Below each destination, a small savings progress bar with a goal amount and current saved amount. A "Start Planning" button visible on the active destination.

**Visual priority:** Aspiration and momentum. This should feel like a vision board, not a spreadsheet.

**Overlay:**
- Headline: "Dream it. Save for it. Go."
- Sub-label: "Set goals. Track savings. Plan when you're ready."

**Purpose:** Show the full lifecycle beginning. The Dream tab positions ROAM as a long-term travel companion, not a single-use itinerary generator.
